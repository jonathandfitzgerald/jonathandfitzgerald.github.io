Hello Mr. Fitzgerald,

My name is Omar Ashmawey and I’m an independent film producer.

First and foremost, I'd like to tell you that I absolutely loved "Not Your Mother's Morals." :) I found it to be very compelling and a fresh take on the concept of morality in our generation.

Given your experience and expertise, I’m contacting you in particular to get your advice on our latest feature length film, Boiling Pot. This independent film stars Danielle Fishel (Boy Meets World), Oscar-winner Lou Gossett Jr, Emmy Award-winner Keith David, Emmet Walsh (Bladerunner), John Heard (Home Alone), and Davetta Sherwood (The Young & The Restless).

The film is based on true events and tells the story of racism on a college campus during the 2008 Presidential election. The plot is centered around modern day racism with a great grassroots campaign and Danielle Fishel in her first serious role in a drama. We’re JUST about to hit the festival circuit and are looking for the right fits.

I’ve been hearing that not every festival is right for every film, and vice versa. Given that you’re the Executive Director for the HFF, I wanted to get your opinion on whether or our film (albeit somewhat controversial and of a heavy subject matter) would be appropriate for this kind of festival.

Is there a specific theme this year? I understand that Cinecause is now looking for more "social-impact" cinema so I would think that Boiling Pot would fall under this category. However, this is our first year at festivals for a full length feature and we’re still learning the ropes. So any advice you may have or any guidance would be most appreciated.

Thank you so much!

PS. I've also emailed you this same message in hopes to reach you. Thanks again for your time.

Omar Ashmawey
