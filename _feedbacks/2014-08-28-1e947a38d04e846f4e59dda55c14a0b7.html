Dear Mr. Fitzgerald,

I've been enjoying several articles on Patrol's website, and your recent article about nature made me think of Richard Wilbur's wonderful poem, "Advice to a Prophet" in which the speaker describes nature as "that live tongue."

http://www.poets.org/poetsorg/poem/advice-prophet

It is nature, says the speaker, through which we humans utter "the rose of our love and the clean / Horse of our courage." We see in nature "The singing locust of the soul unshelled."

The speaker is worried about the destruction of nature because, as your article mentions, we understand ourselves through the creatures and living and still things we did NOT make. How will we explain ourselves to ourselves without nature to be our language?

Thank you for the article.

Sincerely,

Davis

P.S. I am just a public school English teacher in Auburn, Alabama in case you're wondering.
