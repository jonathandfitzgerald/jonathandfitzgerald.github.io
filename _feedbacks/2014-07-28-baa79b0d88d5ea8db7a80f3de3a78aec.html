Hi, my name is Tacita and I am a recent graduate from the MRes programme of the London Consortium, University of London.

I am currently writing an article on The New Sincerity VS. Irony debate and, having read your book "Not Your Mother’s Morals: How the New Sincerity Is Changing Pop Culture for the Better", I was hoping I could get your answers to a couple of questions.

In particular, given the possibility of irony leading to "a sinkhole of relativism and disavowal", do you think irony is degrading contemporary culture? Why and how?

Regarding the earnest backlash of New Sincerity, do you believe it has eroded irony? To which extent and what are the consequences?

Do you believe there is the possibility for this ‘authenticity’ to be commoditised? How?

I would truly value to be able to quote you regarding these points: I appreciate you may not have the time to answer exhaustively or think about each question, but any answer I can get is valuable to me.

Hoping to hear from you, please receive by best regards,
